About

Escape to the tropical paradise of Sri Lanka, where ancient temples, lush rainforests, and pristine beaches await. Immerse yourself in the island's rich cultural heritage, explore vibrant markets, and savor the delectable flavors of local cuisine. From thrilling adventures to serene retreats, Sri Lanka offers something for every traveler. Begin your journey in the ancient city of Anuradhapura, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Marvel at the towering Ruwanweli Maha Seya, one of the largest stupas in the world, and explore the ancient ruins of the Royal Palace. Continue your adventure to Sigiriya, the iconic Lion Rock, where you can climb to the summit for breathtaking panoramic views. For nature lovers, Sri Lanka's national parks offer a diverse range of wildlife. Spot elephants, leopards, and a variety of bird species in Yala National Park. Hike through the misty landscapes of Horton Plains National Park, home to the World's End cliff. Or embark on a safari in Udawalawe National Park to witness the majestic elephants in their natural habitat. Indulge in the island's culinary delights, from spicy curries to aromatic rice and coconut milk dishes. Sample fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and Ceylon tea, a world-renowned specialty. Explore the vibrant markets of Kandy, where you can find colorful handicrafts, spices, and local delicacies. Experience the warmth and hospitality of the Sri Lankan people as you explore this enchanting island. Whether you're seeking adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, Sri Lanka offers an unforgettable experience.

Sri Lanka is a popular tourist destination. Tourism is a key industry that attracts international tourists yearly. Foreigners visit Sri Lanka to see nature, wildlife, historical monuments, and indigenous culture. In 2018, tourist arrivals peaked at 2.5 million, who spent a total of US$5.6 billion in the country. However, the COVID-19 pandemic caused tourist numbers to decrease by 92% in 2020. As of 2022, tourist numbers have not rebounded from the pre-crisis high. The government is attempting to attract foreign investment in the country's tourism industry, which began in earnest after the end of the Sri Lankan Civil War in 2009.

Domestic flights in Sri Lanka are quite limited. So for public transportation, you can choose between train and bus, which are cheap but slow and uncomfortable. The most efficient and convenient way to get around Sri Lanka is by hiring a private car and driver or for shorter distances to take a taxi or tuk-tuk.

  1. Determine Your Travel Style and Interests:

    Adventure Seeker: Consider trekking in the Knuckles Mountain Range, surfing in Weligama, or white-water rafting in Kitulgala. Cultural Enthusiast: Explore ancient cities like Anuradhapura and Polonnaruwa, or immerse yourself in local traditions and festivals. Nature Lover: Visit national parks like Yala or Udawalawe for wildlife safaris, or relax on pristine beaches in the south. Wellness Retreat: Enjoy yoga, meditation, and spa treatments in the hill country.

The Kandy Esala Perahera (the Sri Dalada Perahara procession of Kandy) also known as The Festival of the Tooth is a festival held in July and August in Kandy, Sri Lanka. This historical procession is held annually to pay homage to the Sacred Tooth Relic of Buddha housed at the Sri Dalada Maligawa in Kandy
